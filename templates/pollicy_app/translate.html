{% trans "Introduction" %}

{% trans "The malignant influence of social media, as a ubiquitous and powerful medium of communication in countries all" %} {% trans " over the world, has brought about a growing era of challenges such as hate speech, online violence, fake news," %} {% trans " and other forms of harmful content. From the physical world to the digital realm," %} {% trans " gender-based discrimination persists and thrives through malignant online practices." %}

{% trans " Kenya is no exception, as online harassment targets both prominent women and everyday users." %} {% trans " These harmful behaviors are becoming all too common in our online spaces, perpetuated by social, economic, cultural," %} {% trans " and political structures that echo through the medium of digital networks." %}

{% trans " In order to further understand these malignant practices, we conducted a comprehensive study that involved" %} {% trans " tracking and analyzing the online activities of over 250 women and men candidates on Twitter and Facebook during the 2022" %} {% trans " Kenya general elections specifically, during the campaign and election period. Our methodologies encompassed" %} {% trans " a range of techniques, including lexicon-building focus group discussions, data scraping of publicly available profiles," %} {% trans " qualitative data analysis, and the development of a Machine Learning model" %} {% trans " capable of identifying and categorizing instances of online violence and hate speech in both English and Swahili languages." %}

{% trans "RESEARCH QUESTIONS USED TO GUIDE TOWARDS ACHIEVING THE RESEARCH OBJECTIVE" %}

  • {% trans "How do women politicians in Kenya use social media platforms for campaigning" %} {% trans " during the election period?" %}
  • {% trans "How does the use of social media platforms differ amongst men and women candidates?" %}
  • {% trans "How does online violence against women candidates differ from that experienced" %} {% trans " by men in regards to factors such as the form of violence experienced and frequency" %} {% trans " across platforms?" %}
  • {% trans "What evidence of OVAW-P exists on social media platforms and how does it manifest?" %}
  • {% trans "What is the association between OVAW-P and factors such as their age, frequency of" %} {% trans " social media use, and electoral results?" %}

{% trans "FINDINGS FROM THE STUDY REVEALED THE FOLLOWING" %}

  • {% trans "The use of social media platforms for engaging with voters and constituents by women" %} {% trans " politicians remains low in Kenya. 93 percent of the accounts belonging to men" %} {% trans " candidates were used at least once during 2022 on Facebook compared to 80 percent" %} {% trans " of the women politicians accounts." %}
  • {% trans " On Twitter, 49 percent of accounts belonging to women had less than 5 tweets a month" %} {% trans " during the campaign period." %}
  • {% trans "OVAWP was more prevalent among women candidates than men, especially on Twitter where" %} {% trans " 2 out of 5 women's Twitter accounts monitored experienced sexual harassment." %}
  • {% trans "The attacks against Kenyan women politicians were often focused on personal and sexual" %} {% trans " aspects rather than policies or qualifications." %}
  • {% trans "The cultural and traditional barriers that limit women's participation in politics" %} {% trans " contributed to online violence, and factors such as age and party affiliation also made" %} {% trans " women more vulnerable to harassment and abuse." %}

{% trans "How did women politicians in Kenya use social media" %} {% trans " platforms for campaigning during the general elections?" %}

{% trans "Facebook usage was generally higher across the men candidates with 93.8 percent of" %} {% trans " the accounts belonging to the men candidates being used at least once a week compared" %} {% trans " to the women candidates at 80 percent." %}
{% trans " On Twitter, 51 percent of the women candidates used their account at least once a week" %} {% trans " compared to their male counterparts at 56 percent." %}

{% trans "Is there evidence of OVAW-P? If yes, what are the manifestations of OVAW-P?" %}

  • {% trans "OVAW-P was more prevalent among women candidates on Facebook with at least one in two of" %} {% trans " the women's accounts monitored (55.7 percent) experiencing some form of OVAWP compared to" %} {% trans " 35.4 percent among the men candidates." %}
  • {% trans "Additionally, each of the forms of violence observed was more prevalent towards those women" %} {% trans " candidates, especially sexual violence which was predominantly experienced by women candidates." %}
  • {% trans "On Twitter, online violence disproportionately affected women, with significantly higher percentages" %} {% trans " of women experiencing sexual harassment, hate speech, and disinformation compared to men." %}

{% trans "According to the data from Facebook, sexual violence increased with an increase in age."%} {% trans " While the two forms of violence: insult and hate speech and trolling were more associated with younger women age groups." %} {% trans " Online violence was predominantly associated with the losing candidates, and 72.7 percent of the women who lost the election" %} {% trans " experienced disinformation and 60 percent of the women who lost the election experienced insults and hate speech," %} {% trans " and 75% of the women who lost the election experienced sexual harassment and 77.4 percent of the women who lost the" %} {% trans " election experienced trolling." %} {% trans " In terms of social media frequency of use, higher usage of social media platforms resulted in higher levels of online violence." %}
